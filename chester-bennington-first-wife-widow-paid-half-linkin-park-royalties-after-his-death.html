Why Does Chester Bennington Get Half Of His Royalty Payments?

After Linkin Park released their album "Hybrid Theory" in the year 2000, the band became an absolute sensation. Far from a one-hit wonder, Linkin Park would release seven albums and a long list of their songs would become massive hits.

On top of releasing so many albums, Linkin Park became known for their amazing live performances which highlighted how talented they were. Thanks to how much success Linkin Park enjoyed and how much many fans adored them, Chester Bennington will always be remembered as the band's vocalist.

While it seems abundantly clear that Bennington was very proud of everything Linkin Park accomplished, he also worked with other artists. During Bennington's life, he recorded music with bands like Stone Temple Pilots, Grey Daze, and Dead by Sunrise. Bennington also collaborated with many other musical artists including Jay-Z and Chris Cornell.

As a result of all the bands and artists that Bennington worked with over the years, his estate has many revenue streams. With that in mind, it begs an obvious question, where does the money that Bennington's estate bring in go?

When Bennington passed away in 2017, the beloved singer sadly left behind a lot of people who loved him dearly. While it is impossible to list the names of everyone who adored Bennington here, the most obvious people are the singer's children and wives.

In 1996, Bennington became a father for the first time when his girlfriend Elka Brand gave birth to his son Jaime Bennington. Bennington also adopted Brand's child from a previous relationship.

Bennington would then go on to marry Samantha Olit in 1996 and the pair would be married until their divorce was finalized in 2005. During his first marriage, Olit gave birth to Bennington's third child, Draven Bennington.

A year later, Bennington married Talinda Bennington in 2006. Together until Bennington's tragic passing, the rock star and his widow welcomed three children into the world including their twins who were born in 2006.

When Bennington passed away, it seemed very clear that he wanted to make sure his children were all loved. According to TMZ, Bennington's will stipulated that he wanted his six children to spend time together regularly after he was gone.

Given the success Bennington enjoyed, he also left behind a lot of money which easily could have spawned a bitter legal battle. Thankfully, there were never any headlines about his loved ones taking each other to court.

However, it was revealed that Bennington's first wife filed a claim with his estate seeking part of his royalty payments and child support. From the outside looking in, it seems that Bennington's ex-wife and widow quickly came to an agreement without any fighting.

In 2019, it was announced that Bennington's ex-wife and widow reached an agreement over his royalty payments. That agreement stipulated that Bennington's ex-wife would get half of all royalty payments that the singer's estate would receive. Bennington's ex-wife also dropped any attempt to also receive child support as part of the deal.

One thing remains unclear for Bennington's fans who are interested in how his children will be taken care of. As of the time of this writing, it is unclear if Bennington's two children from his relationship with his ex-girlfriend Elka Brand receive part of his royalty payments.
