Channel 7 axes House Rules and Million Dollar Minute

May 17, 2024
blog

CHANNEL SEVEN has shelved a new series of House Rules and is set to dump quiz show Million Dollar Minute.

The moves come as Seven program chief Angus Ross promises to “unleash hell” on rivals Nine and Ten with a swag of new programs in September.

THE BEST OF TV IN SEPTEMBER

Mr Ross has revealed that a new series of House Rules, already filmed and originally slated to screen in late 2015, won’t go to air this year.

He has also confirmed that quiz show Million Dollar Minute is set to be dumped from the 5.30pm timeslot.

Seven will launch a massive slate of new programs over the next month, headed by Peter Allen: The Boy Next Door, Erik Thomson drama 800 Words, and the return of The X Factor, with new judges Chris Isaak and James Blunt joining Dannii Minogue and Guy Sebastian.

New quiz show The Chase Australia, hosted by Andrew O’Keefe, will go into the 5pm to 6pm timeslot, replacing Million Dollar Minute and repeats of Deal or No Deal.

Seven will also fast track new US dramas Heroes Reborn, Quantico and The Playerk, and roll out observational series Beach Cops, Gold Coast Medical and What Really Happens in Thailand.

The salvo of new shows comes as Seven seeks to recover from the failure of Restaurant Revolution.

“We are about to unleash hell in the final quarter,” Mr Ross says. “You always want to come home with a wet sail at the end of the year. We have obviously had a show (Restaurant Revolution) that has disappointed for us.”

Mr Ross says House Rules, hosted by Johanna Griggs, was shelved because it would inevitably go up against the new series of The Block on Nine in the 7.30pm timeslot.

Seven has already suffered from copycat crunch with the May series of House Rules cruelled by rival Reno Rumble.

“We’ve got a series there (completed) but I don’t think the genre matching that has occurred this year has helped anyone,” Mr Ross says.

“Do I think House Rules versus The Block is going to be a good outcome for us or for audiences? No. I don’t think it would be a win for anyone.”

Million Dollar Minute is being replaced because it simply hasn’t rated strongly enough in the crucial 5.30pm timeslot, dominated by Nine’s Hot Seat.

The show has had a chequered history since it launched in September 2013. Original host Grant Denyer quit the following November and was replaced by Simon Reeve.

“I always want shows to win their timeslot … and it hasn’t done that unfortunately (so) we need to rectify that,” Mr Ross says.

“We believe there is a demand for entertainment in that hour (5pm to 6pm). The Chase is really good fun and great to play along with.”

The two-part Peter Allen: The Boy Next Door has been rushed to air. It was originally scheduled to screen next February.

The miniseries stars Joel Jackson as Allen with Sigrid Thornton as Judy Garland, Sara West as Liza Minnelli, and Rebecca Gibney as Allen’s mother Marion Woolnough.

The Molly miniseries, with Samuel Johnson as music guru Molly Meldrum, will now go into that February slot.

“This is a big roll of the dice for Seven, but we believe it will revitalise the network and restore faith in their programming prowess,” media analyst Steve Allen says.

Seven won’t have it easy. Nine and Ten have plenty of ammunition left. Ten is set to launch The Bachelorette and The Biggest Loser Families. Its Bachelor finale is sure to rate big.

Nine will roll out another Celebrity Apprentice Australia. It also has high hopes for its stripped down version of The Block, set at an eight-floor motel in South Yarra.

House Husbands will continue to the end of the ratings year. There are new episodes of The Big Bang Theory, RBT and Kings Cross ER on the way too.

“I think we’ve got a very strong last quarter,” Nine program chief Andrew Backwell says.

“In the past, we’ve limped to the end of survey and blown all our content in the first half. (This year) we have balanced our schedule better.”
