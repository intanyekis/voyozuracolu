Christie Ann Hefner Wiki Biography

Christie Ann Hefner, born on November 8, 1952, in Chicago, Illinois USA, is the former executive director and chairman of the board of Playboy Enterprises, a position she held from 1982 to 2009. The company was founded by her father Hugh Hefner. She currently serves as the Chairman of the Board of Directors of Hatch Beauty and a board member and strategic advisor of Luminary Digital Media. She is also a member of the editorial advisory board global for Reuters.

Christie Hefner’s net worth is estimated to be as much as $20 million, according to authoritative sources, as of late 2016. Playboy Enterprises is the main source of her wealth, although she has earned sums from other engagements.

Christie Hefner Net Worth $20 Million

Christie is the daughter of Mildred Williams and Hugh Hefner. Her parents separated when she was five. After her mother remarried, the family moved to Wilmette, Illinois, where she graduated from New Trier High School, and later attended Brandeis University, earning a Bachelor’s degree in English and American Literature in 1974.

She joined Playboy right after graduation. Within four years, she rose to the position of vice-president, and in 1982, she became the president of Playboy Enterprises, eventually also serving as the president of the board and CEO from 1988. Under her leadership, the company acquired other adult-oriented businesses such as Spice Network and ClubJenna. In 2008, she announced plans to resign from her CEO post as of January 31, 2009, stating her inspiration from Barack Obama’s presidency to focus on charity work. In 2011, she was named the CEO of Canyon Ranch Enterprises.

Her philanthropic efforts include launching the First Amendment Award in honor of her father Hugh Hefner and helping to raise $30 million to build the CORE Center in Chicago, the first facility for people with AIDS in the Midwestern United States.

In her personal life, Christie Hefner married former Illinois senator William A. Marovitz in 1995, but they have no children. The couple separated in 2011 and divorced in 2013. Marovitz was sued by the USA Securities and Exchange Commission in 2012 for alleged use of insider information for illegal trade in shares of Playboy, a matter settled out of court.
